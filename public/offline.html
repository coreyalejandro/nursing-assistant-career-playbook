Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) Career Playbook

You're offline — here's guidance that works without a connection.
Feeling overwhelmed or in crisis?

You can call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), 24/7, free and confidential. You matter, and support is available right now.

CNA certification basics (federal)

  • Complete a state-approved training program — federal minimum 75 hours (16+ clinical). Many states require more.
  • Pass the competency exam: a written/oral test and a hands-on skills demonstration.
  • Clear a background check and health screening (e.g., TB test).
  • Get listed on your state's Nurse Aide Registry before working for pay.
  • Renew every 24 months with proof of paid nursing-related work.

Protect your license & residents

  • Never share resident names, room numbers, or record numbers outside care needs (HIPAA).
  • Stay within your scope: report changes in condition to your supervising nurse — don't give medical advice or medication.
  • Document at the point of care, accurately and on time.

Quick interview prep (STAR)

  • Situation → Task → Action → Result.
  • Prepare one story each: de-escalation, a fall prevented, a teamwork win, a hard family conversation.
